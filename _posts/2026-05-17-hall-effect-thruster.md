## Background
I have been a member of the experimental propulsion club at ASU for the last three semesters. I helped in the design and construction of our first project which was a magneto hydrodynamic drive. My contributions to the magnetohydrodynamic drive were minimal as my class and work schedule did not align well with the club's schedule. I was able to get more time off of work to help finish the project. This last semester I was elected to be the electronics and software teamlead. This semester our club has focused on the design of a Hall Effect thruster. A Hall effect thruster is a type of ion thruster that confines electrons with a magnetic field so that positively charged ions can be accelerated towards said electrons and ejected together from the thruster thereby generating thrust. Hall Effect thrusters are incredibly efficient in comparison to the chemical counterparts. They are best used on spacecraft with long duration missions. A notable example of a space craft that utilizes a Hall Effect thruster are the starlink satellites.
