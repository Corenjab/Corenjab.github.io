## Background
I have been a member of the experimental propulsion club at ASU for the last three semesters. I helped in the design and construction of our first project which was a magneto hydrodynamic drive. My contributions to the magnetohydrodynamic drive were minimal as my class and work schedule did not align well with the club's schedule. I was able to get more time off of work to help finish the project. This last semester I was elected to be the electronics and software teamlead. This semester our club has focused on the design of a Hall Effect thruster. A Hall effect thruster is a type of ion thruster that confines electrons with a magnetic field so that positively charged ions can be accelerated towards said electrons and ejected together from the thruster thereby generating thrust. Hall Effect thrusters are incredibly efficient in comparison to their chemical counterparts. They are best used on spacecraft with long duration missions. A notable example of a space craft that utilizes a Hall Effect thruster are the starlink satellites. 


## Overview
The electrical team was tasked with designing and making a power supply and sensors to power and monitor a Hall Effect Thruster.
There are three main components of the hall effect thruster that need power: the cathode and anode; the keeper; and the electromagnets. 
The current between the cathode and anode is what supplies the charges that are used to generate our thrust. Therfore the thrust number of charges and therfore the thrust is directly proportional to the current supplied. The keeper is there to start the flow of charges and keep it hence its name. The electromagnets are needed to generate the field that enables the Hall current that causes the confinement of the electrons. The electrical team, under my direction, has been working closely with the other subteams in the club to design a power supply that will meet the needs of the components that they are designing. Over the course of this last semester the electrical team has has grown a lot in its capabilities in order to meet the design challenge it has been faced with. Attached is our current documentation and designs. Ther is still a lot we need to work on, but we will get it done.
